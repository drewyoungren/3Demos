Suppose a mass distribution is given by density function \(\rho(x,y,z)\) over a region \(E\). Its moment of inertia relative to an axis of rotation is the integral of the square of the distance to the axis times a mass element. These are "second" moments and measure the "spread" of a distribution. For example, the moment of inertia about the \(z\)-axis is $$I = \iiint_E (x^2 + y^2) \rho(x,y,z)\,dV $$.

If $\omega$ is the angular velocity (in radians/unit time), then the angular momentum $$L = I\omega $$ is conserved.
