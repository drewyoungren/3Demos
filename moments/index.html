Suppose a mass distribution is given by density function \(\rho(x,y,z)\) over a region \(E\). Mass is density times volume so we can write the "mass element" \(dM = \rho\,dV\) which we integrate to get the total mass. This is the "0th moment." $$ M = \iiint_E \rho(x,y,z)\,dV $$

The center of mass is computed from the "1st moments" and are, essentially, weighted averages of each coordinate. \begin{align*} \bar{x} &= \frac{1}{M} \iiint_E x\,\rho(x,y,z)\,dV \\ \bar{y} &= \frac{1}{M} \iiint_E y\,\rho(x,y,z)\,dV \\ \bar{z} &= \frac{1}{M} \iiint_E z\,\rho(x,y,z)\,dV \\ \end{align*}
