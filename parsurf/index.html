A parametric surface $\Sigma$ is the image of a continuous, vector-valued function of two variables (parameters).

$$\mathbf r(u,v) = \begin{bmatrix} x(u,v) \\ y(u,v) \\ z(u,v) \end{bmatrix} $$ for $(u,v)$ in some domain $ D \subset \mathbb{R}^2$.

$$\mathbf r(u,v) = \begin{bmatrix} x(u,v) \\ y(u,v) \\ z(u,v) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} u \\ v \\ f(u,v) \end{bmatrix} $$ where $u,v$ are input parameters and $f(u,v)$ gives 3rd coordinate.

$$\mathbf r(u,v) = \begin{bmatrix} x(u,v) \\ y(u,v) \\ z(u,v) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} u \\ R(u) \cos(v) \\ R(u) \sin(v) \end{bmatrix} $$ where $u$ tracks position along axis. $R(u)$ is the radius at position $u$.$v$ tracks angle around axis.

$$\mathbf r(u,v) = \begin{bmatrix} x(u,v) \\ y(u,v) \\ z(u,v) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} R\sin(u)\cos(v) \\ R\sin(u)\sin(v) \\ R\cos(u) \end{bmatrix} $$ where $u$ is angle $\phi$, $v$ is angle $\theta$, and $R$ is the fixed radius.
