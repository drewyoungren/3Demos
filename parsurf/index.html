⊕ Parametric Surfaces

A parametric surface $\Sigma$ is the image of a continuous, vector-valued function of two variables (parameters). $$\mathbf r(u,v) = \begin{bmatrix} x(u,v) \\ y(u,v) \\ z(u,v) \end{bmatrix} $$ for $(u,v)$ in some domain $D \subset \mathbb{R}^2$. A surface is smooth if $\mathbf r$ is continuously differentiable and has at each point a nonzero normal vector $$\mathbf n = \mathbf r_u \times \mathbf r_v. $$ We concentrate on 3 classes of surfaces.
