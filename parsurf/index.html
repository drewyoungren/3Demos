⊕ Parametric Surfaces

A parametric surface $\Sigma$ is the image of a continuous, vector-valued function of two variables (parameters). $$\mathbf r(u,v) = \begin{bmatrix} x(u,v) \\ y(u,v) \\ z(u,v) \end{bmatrix} $$ for $(u,v)$ in some domain $D \subset \mathbb{R}^2$.

Classes of surfaces:

graphs of functions   |   surfaces of revolution  
  parts of spheres   |   custom

\begin{align} x(u,v) &= u \\ y(u,v) &= v \\ z(u,v) &= f(u,v) \end{align}

$u,v$ are input parameters. $f(u,v)$ gives 3rd coordinate.

\begin{align} x(u,v) &= u \\ y(u,v) &= R(u) \cos(v) \\ z(u,v) &= R(u) \sin(v) \end{align}

$u$ tracks position along axis. $R(u)$ is the radius at position $u$.$v$ tracks angle around axis.

\begin{align} x(u,v) &= R\sin(u)\cos(v) \\ y(u,v) &= R\sin(u)\sin(v) \\ z(u,v) &= R\cos(u) \end{align}

$u$ is angle $\phi$. $v$ is angle $\theta$. $R$ is the fixed radius.
