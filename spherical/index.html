Rectangular \(x,y,z\), Cylindrical \((r,\theta,z)\), and spherical coordinates \((\rho,\theta,\phi)\) specify a point in 3-space by the formulae: \begin{align*} x &= r \cos \theta &= \rho \sin \phi \cos \theta \\ y &= r \sin \theta &= \rho \sin \phi \sin \theta \\ z &= z &= \rho \cos \phi \\ \end{align*}

That is,

  1. \(r\) measures distance from the $z$-axis,
  2. \(\rho\) measures distance from the origin,
  3. \(\theta\) measures angle in radians off the positive \(x\)-axis counterclockwise around the $z$-axis, and
  4. \(\phi\) measures angle off the positive $z$-axis.
We thus have the important relations $r^2 = x^2 + y^2$ and $\rho^2 = x^2 + y^2 + z^2$.

A region described by small intervals $\Delta \rho, \Delta \theta, \Delta \phi$ of coordinate values has a volume roughly $$\Delta \rho (\rho \sin \phi \Delta \theta) (\rho \Delta \phi).$$

This gives the important result $$dV = \rho^2\sin \theta\,d\rho\,d\theta\,d\phi$$ for writing triple integrals in spherical coordinates.
